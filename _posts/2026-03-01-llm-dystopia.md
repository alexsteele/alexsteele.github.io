What LLM applications are most dystopian?

First, what are they good at?

1. LLMs are tremendous at searching and summarizing written text.
2. LLMs are often superhuman at tasks they have been trained on, tasks with a
   clear evaluation metric and training examples.
3. LLMs are OK but not great at writing human-like text. They have a
   characteristic style.

What are the most worrisome use cases?

1. **Surveillance** - Monitoring, filtering, flagging, and analyzing human communications.
   1. Find text messages indicating these beliefs, breakdown these political
      beliefs by zip-code.
   2. Monitor my employees' computers and flag employees doing these things.
      Summarize what they are writing about outside of their job duties and
      identify troublemakers.
2. **Judgement** - Substituting human judgement in hiring, law, weapons
   targeting, and other high-impact evaluations. LLMs dramatically reduce time
   and cost, and thus it is tempting to substitute computer judgement for human
   judgement.
3. **Recommendations** - Guiding recommendation and ranking systems in social
   media feeds, search, and advertising based on fuzzy criteria. LLMs do best as
   classifiers and sentiment analysis, translating goals like "less ragebait"
   into ranking signals and filtering inbound content.
4. **Garbage** - Generating a huge volume of plausible-looking garbage text and
   images, for example to flood Reddit with fake but semi-real-seeming content.

In effect, any research task based on text which you might have hired a human to
do, you can now have an LLM make a pretty good pass at with a human to double
check, at superhuman speed and a fraction of the cost.

You now might deploy a small team of people+LLMs to do research and analysis on
populations that previously required hundreds of analysts, or was simply
unfeasible to do, such as analyzing emerging belief patterns along certain axis
from online activity in an entire country. A single software engineer with
access to a 1TB text dump and a powerful LLM can process and summarize it along
many dimensions in a few hours.

This can then be steered back into search, ranking, and recommendation systems
to shape opinion. LLMs are good at identifying content meeting certain criteria.
If you control the feeds, a small team of people with these tools can filter and
steer the content people are exposed to.
